# Bishorgo Experience — Content Strategy & Website Copy Guideline

## 0. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the complete content direction for the Bishorgo Experience website.

It is written for:

- Founders
- Copywriters
- Designers
- Developers
- AI/coding agents
- Content strategists
- Social media team members

The goal is to make sure every word on the Bishorgo Experience website feels strategic, human, premium, Bangladeshi, and experience-driven.

The website must not sound like a normal digital marketing agency website.

Bishorgo Experience is not simply selling websites, social media, branding, or campaigns.

Bishorgo Experience is selling memory.

The core idea:

> People forget advertisements. People remember experiences.

Everything on the website must support this idea.

---

# 1. Brand Content Positioning

## 1.1 What Bishorgo Experience Is

Bishorgo Experience is a creative growth company that helps brands become memorable through strategy, branding, web development, content, marketing, field activation, and customer experience design.

It should be positioned as:

> A brand experience company built for Bangladeshi businesses that want to be remembered.

Do not position Bishorgo as only:

- A digital marketing agency
- A web development agency
- A social media agency
- A branding agency
- A creative agency

Those are services.

The category is bigger:

> Brand Experience Company

## 1.2 Core Belief

Use this belief everywhere internally:

> A business does not live in its logo, website, or Facebook page. A business lives in the memory of its customers.

## 1.3 Core Message

Primary message:

> People forget campaigns. People remember experiences.

Alternative versions:

- People forget ads. People remember how a brand made them feel.
- A brand is not remembered by being seen once. It is remembered by creating a moment.
- Your customers do not only need to notice you. They need to remember you.
- We turn brand interactions into experiences people remember.
- We help businesses become unforgettable.

## 1.4 One-Line Description

Use this short description wherever needed:

> Bishorgo Experience helps businesses create memorable customer experiences through branding, technology, content, marketing, and real-world activations.

## 1.5 Extended Description

Use this for About, decks, website introduction, or profile descriptions:

> Bishorgo Experience is a Bangladesh-based brand experience company helping businesses turn ordinary customer touchpoints into memorable moments. We combine strategy, branding, web development, content, social media, field marketing, and customer experience design to help brands stay in the minds of their consumers.

# 2. Content Personality

## 2.1 Voice

The Bishorgo voice should be:

- Strategic
- Human
- Observational
- Founder-focused
- Culturally aware
- Clear
- Confident
- Premium, but not cold
- Creative, but not childish

## 2.2 Tone

The tone should feel like a sharp strategist talking to a business owner.

It should not sound like:

- A generic agency
- A motivational speaker
- A corporate brochure
- A tech startup using buzzwords
- A social media caption factory

## 2.3 Tone Formula

Use this formula:

> Simple language + strong insight + human business reality + clear next step

Example:

Bad:

> We provide innovative digital marketing solutions to accelerate your growth.

Good:

> Your customers are seeing hundreds of brands every day. We help yours become the one they remember.

# 3. Language Selection

## 3.1 Primary Website Language

Primary language:

> English

Reason:

- Gives premium positioning
- Works for founders, startups, corporates, and international visitors
- Helps Bishorgo look strategic, not small/local-only

## 3.2 Secondary Language

Secondary language:

> Bangla / Banglish

Use Bangla selectively for emotional impact, not for every section.

Bangla should be used in:

- Small emotional lines
- Campaign examples
- Local market observations
- Blog/social content
- Experience Lab examples
- Bangladeshi founder-focused moments

## 3.3 Language Rule

Do not randomly mix Bangla and English inside serious website copy.

Use Bangla only when it adds emotional or cultural meaning.

Example:

Good:

> In Bangladesh, a business is not only judged by what it sells. It is judged by how people feel after interacting with it.

Good Bangla emotional line:

> মনে থাকাটাই আসল জায়গা।

Bad:

> We create memorable অভিজ্ঞতা for brands with awesome solutions.

# 4. Words to Use and Avoid

## 4.1 Words to Use Often

Use these words because they fit the Bishorgo positioning:

- Experience
- Memory
- Moment
- Touchpoint
- Customer journey
- Brand recall
- Human connection
- Business growth
- Strategy
- Identity
- Presence
- Trust
- Attention
- Relationship
- Culture
- Local insight
- Execution
- Measurable
- Memorable
- Real-world
- Digital presence

## 4.2 Words to Avoid

Avoid these unless absolutely necessary:

- Innovative solutions
- Full-service agency
- 360-degree solution
- One-stop solution
- Cutting-edge
- Result-oriented
- Best digital marketing agency
- Grow your business online
- Boost your brand
- Creative excellence
- Dynamic team
- Unique solutions
- We provide services
- Affordable package

These words are overused in agency websites.

## 4.3 Replacement Guide

Instead of:

> We provide digital marketing services.

Use:

> We design the moments that make people notice, trust, and remember your brand.

Instead of:

> We are a full-service creative agency.

Use:

> We bring strategy, design, technology, and field execution together to create brand experiences that stay with people.

Instead of:

> Contact us for your business growth.

Use:

> Show us your business. We will show you where the experience can become stronger.

# 5. Website Content Architecture

The website should follow this content journey:

1. Create curiosity
2. Explain the belief
3. Show the method
4. Prove the thinking
5. Offer services
6. Capture leads
7. Keep visitors inside the ecosystem

Do not start with a service list.

The visitor should first understand Bishorgo’s worldview.

## 6.6 Case Study Page

### Purpose

Case studies should prove Bishorgo’s thinking, not just show images.

### Required Structure

1. Client / project name
2. Industry
3. Problem
4. Insight
5. Strategy
6. Execution
7. Experience created
8. Results
9. Learnings
10. CTA

### Case Study Template

#### Problem

> What was the business struggling with?

#### Insight

> What customer truth did we find?

#### Strategy

> What did we decide to change and why?

#### Execution

> What was created across brand, web, content, marketing, or field activation?

#### Experience

> What did customers actually feel, see, do, or remember?

#### Result

> What changed after the work?

### Result Metrics

Use real numbers whenever possible:

- Website visits
- Leads generated
- Sales inquiries
- Engagement rate
- Footfall
- Brand recall
- Event participation
- Conversion rate
- Customer feedback

If no numbers are available, use qualitative outcomes honestly.

## 6.7 Blog / Insights Page

### Purpose

Blog content should educate founders and build SEO authority.

It should not be random content marketing spam.

### Blog Categories

- Brand Strategy
- Customer Experience
- Web Development
- Social Media
- Field Marketing
- Founder Growth
- Bangladesh Business Insights
- Experience Design
- Local Marketing

### Blog Tone

Blog posts should be:

- Useful
- Clear
- Practical
- Observational
- Locally relevant
- Easy to read

### Blog Length

Recommended:

- Short insight: 600–900 words
- Deep guide: 1,200–2,000 words
- Case study: 800–1,500 words
- Founder note: 500–1,000 words

### Blog Opening Rule

Start with a real business situation, not a definition.

Bad:

> Branding is the process of creating a unique identity for a business.

Good:

> A customer walks past ten shops before entering one. The difference is not always price. Sometimes the business simply feels easier to trust.

# 11. AI / Coding Agent Content Brain

Any AI or coding agent working on the website must follow this content brain.

## 11.1 Agent Role

You are building the website for Bishorgo Experience, a Bangladesh-based brand experience company. The website itself must feel like an experience. Every section must support the idea that brands are remembered through meaningful customer touchpoints.

## 11.2 Agent Writing Rules

The agent must:

- Write clearly
- Avoid generic agency language
- Use experience and memory as central themes
- Keep copy human and strategic
- Prioritize business owners and founders
- Avoid unnecessary buzzwords
- Keep CTAs action-driven
- Use English primarily, with selective Bangla when emotionally useful
- Maintain premium but approachable tone

## 11.3 Agent Must Avoid

The agent must not write:

- We provide innovative solutions
- We are a full-service digital marketing agency
- We help brands grow online
- Boost your business with our services
- Contact us today for the best service
- Lorem ipsum style filler copy

## 11.4 Agent Should Prefer

The agent should write:

- We help businesses become memorable
- Every brand touchpoint should create a memory
- Your website is often the first serious experience customers have with your brand
- We build systems that connect branding, content, technology, and real-world activation

# 15. Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing any website content, check:

- Does it support the idea of memorable experiences?
- Is the language simple and human?
- Does it avoid generic agency jargon?
- Is the CTA clear?
- Does it speak to founders/business owners?
- Does it explain business value, not just services?
- Does it feel premium but not robotic?
- Is the copy easy to scan?
- Are headings strong?
- Does it include local relevance where useful?
- Is the SEO title and meta description ready?
- Is the content mobile-friendly?

# 16. Final Content Principle

Every page should answer one question:

> Why should this business trust Bishorgo to make their brand more memorable?

If the content does not answer that, rewrite it.

Bishorgo Experience is not here to add more noise.
